GEN WISE LANGUAGE HUB

Grammar: Present Simple with "to be"

Structure:

Subject + am/is/are + complement

Usage:

Describes a state, emotion, or fact in the present. Often used in greetings to express emotions or describe someone.

Examples:

  • I am happy to meet you. (Tôi vui khi gặp bạn.)
  • She is my friend. (Cô ấy là bạn của tôi.)
  • We are nice people. (Chúng tôi là người tốt.)

Reading Comprehension

A New Friend
Hello! My name is Mai. Today is a good day. I meet a new friend at school. His name is Nam. He has a nice smile. We hit it off quickly. I say, “Yo, Nam! Let’s be friends!” Nam is happy and says, “Yes, I wish we have fun!” We talk and laugh. It’s easy to break the ice with him. I say, “Goodbye, Nam! See you tomorrow!” He says, “Have a good day, Mai!” I feel happy.

Questions:

  • 1. What is Mai’s new friend’s name?
  • 2. What does Mai say to Nam to become friends?
  • 3. How does Nam feel?
